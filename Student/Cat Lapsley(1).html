Colony collapse and the back up plan

My Opinion:  Colony collapse is a problem and it must be solved.What is colony collapse? It’s when most of the worker bees leave  behind the larva food and a few nurse bees to care for them. Pesticides may be why this is happening.  To help, don't use them near flowers or bee farmers. I know that many farmers use pesticides on their crops and would definitely argue with me.

     There has to be a backup plan if they do die out. Honey wasps produce honey in large amounts. Don't forget,  they pollinate flowers in early summer.  They are found in Mexico and South Texas and Central America. But that would mean bringing  them to the rest of the US.  I have more to say. The honey wasp is being tested to see how similar they are to honey bee. These wasps normally ignore human activity.  We would have to take them from the places they’re found. Plus we would have to invade the space we were keeping them in. In my opinion, this really isn't a good backup plan. But if we don't save the bees, this is what it might come to.

      Honey bees sure pollinate a lot. Yes. In fact they're responsible for over 70 percent of pollinating. Other pollinators do exist. But bees do most of it and also are one of the things that makes honey. But because of (ccd) the bees are dying. The stuff they pollinate is livestock food. With no bees means less livestock  food and food less food for the livestock which means less quality livestock. Dairy would be more limited. Milk cows wouldn't make as much milk.
