The Abuse of Drugs in America

“Drugs take you to hell,Disguised as Heaven”  In America there are problems… murders,riots,rape, and people in poverty. The list just goes on and on and on, but drugs is close to the top of the list. The abuse of drugs legal and illegal in America is so high, but goes by unnoticed by so many. You hear these stories about mothers,fathers,sisters, and brothers who have died to drugs, by overdosing and no one was there to help them.”The police don’t help, a decent rehab cost thousands of dollars and that is just for a decent one. Even then rehabs may not work.” (https://kleantreatmentcenters.com,No clear author) America is a practically letting this happen. ‘Currently there is a war on drugs going on in America and it has been going on for almost 40 years now. Since, No Clear Author) This supposed war on drugs has had no impact on the problem with drugs and it is not acceptable.

The War on Drugs was started in 1971 by President Richard Milhous Nixon and has continued for 36 years now. Technically the longest war America has ever been in. At the beginning of the war, 1.3 % of America’s population was addicted to drugs. In 2016 the percentage of people addicted to drugs in the U.S. was 1.1%.’(https://web.stanford.edu, No Clear Author) Now even though the number has gone down (if only by .2 percent), it technically has not because  America’s population in 1971 was a lot smaller than America’s population in 2016. That means that 1.1% is actually bigger in 2016 than 1.3% in 1971 when it comes to population.

From the information above we can deduce that the War on Drugs or the Drug War has no impact on the problem on drugs. “Every year we spend over 35 billion on the supposed Drug war and since the beginning of the war we have spent over a trillion dollars.” (www.drugpolicy.org, No Clear Author) Why would the U.S. spend billions on something that is not working. A well know world wide quote, “Doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results.” That is what America is doing. The only real effect the Drug War has had is over-population in jails and prisons from drug charges and guess what. People still manage to overdose in jail, even though jail is suppose to be the one place that people shouldn’t be able to get drugs. This should not happen.

        As you may know, there is overpopulation in jails and prisons all across America. Let’s put this into a little bit more understandable terms. There's a little over 2.3 million people incarcerated in America to this day. “ 1 in 5 incarcerated people are locked up for a drug offense.” (www.prisonpolicy.org, No Known Author) That means if we take the whatever exact number of people incarcerated and round that number to 2.3 million and then divide it by 5 we get 460,000 people in Jail or Prison for a drug offense. That’s about 21.1% of the 2.3million. Almost a quarter of the total population of incarcerated people in U.S.. Now let's get a little more specific to just Prisons since the beginning of 2017. ‘So far 176,655 people have been incarcerated in 2017. Of this 175,655 people, 81,847 have been incarcerated for a drug offense.’(www.bop.gov, No Known Author) That's 45.3% of the prisons population this year. ‘ The close’s number for a charges is 29,782 people for Weapon,Arson, and Explosives.’ (https://www.bop.gov., No Known Author) This is the only other offense in the double percentage at 16.9% The number of people incarcerated for a drug offense is slowly increasing.

Well for those who believe that marijuana is some gateway drug into even harder drugs, you are wrong and there is no such thing as harder drugs. All drugs are bad for you. Back to the whole marijuana is a gateway drug thing, a study has shown that marijuana has never caused users to seek “ harder drugs or anything like that.” (https://web.stanford.edu, No Known Author) Others May ask what else can America do, we are trying are “best”, but there is other solutions.

In Berne,Switzerland, 1986 the first Safe Injection Site was created because,

“People then began using these cafes as places to take drugs and the authorities decided that, rather than banning the practice, it would be more effective to monitor it and provide users with a safe and clean environment” (http://www.bbc.com, No Known author)

You see instead of shunning users/addicts and trying to help them by not banning use of drugs, but making as safe as possible for them even though it is still is bad for them. If you still do not believe this is effective then look. Since Switzerland Canada, The Netherlands, Australia, and Germany have all done this in their countries and it has worked.

        America has to stop the War on Drugs and start a new route. As data has shown that the Drug War Isn't helping. All it has done is cost billions. No. Over a trillion dollars on nothing and overpopulation in jails and Prisons. The U.S. needs to stop shunning addicts/users and start trying to actually help them.
