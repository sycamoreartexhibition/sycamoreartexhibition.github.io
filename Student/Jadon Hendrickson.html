The California Drought

Right now it’s 2017. And weather patterns have shown that water is returning to California after a long harsh drought. And while we think all of our problems are solved, they aren’t entirely. If we get ready for the next drought, we won’t need to worry about running out of water so fast. Or having to solely pray for water to return.

        Now, while preparation is the way to go in terms of the drought, why should we? According to www.sciencenews.org, Thomas Sumer, 2014 Dec. 6th, there hasn’t been a drought this bad since the year 800! That’s over 1,200 years ago. But then there’s people who say, “We weren’t here during the year 800!” True, but scientists have been able to count the rings of Blue Oak trees that are from about the year 800. And they’ve only died now. So according to the trees, we will die before the next severe drought. So what do we care?

        This all makes total sense. What’s the point of solving a problem that won’t happen again in our lifetime? And do we really need to help our descendants? According to www.NASA.gov, NASA CO., 2014 Dec. 16, we lost trillions of gallons of water. And according to NASA, it was going to take 11 trillion gallons of water to replenish all we had lost. But now, this 2017, we have been declared out of the drought by our governor, Jerry Brown. We are still technically in drought but not in an emergency standpoint. So we at least know that our water is gonna return to us in one form or another for us when needed! Right?

        Well, it sounds kind of cruel to leave descendants hanging like that with no water or substitutes. But even so, what’s not to say the next horrible drought won’t happen sooner?  And what if we lose more than 11 trillion gallons of water? What if we lose 20 trillion gallons next time!? That’s why we need to consider what has happened, what does happen, and what we can have happen. Just like how devastating the drought was in 2015, what CA produces, and reverse osmosis.

        First, in order to prepare ourselves for the next drought, we must consider what has happened. The worst of the California drought was in 2015 and everyone was fearing for their lives about the water. To make matters worse, according to www.latimes.com, article by Kyle Kim and Thomas Suh Lauder, 2017, March 23, there was the most drought in the valley in 2014-2015. And, only 1 year of water reserves remained in 2015 before the water would run out. Also, the drought map images were actually taken from the good website, www.droughtmonitor.unl.edu. They not only have information on the current maps regarding the California drought, but also droughts around America. And their results would show us Californians the horrifying truth as the drought got worse throughout the years. That’s what we must know first before we jump to today.

        Now, in order to be ready for the next drought, we must realize what California actually does. California produces 83% of the US’s frozen strawberries for one thing. California also produces 25% of our onions and 43% of all our green onions too! Without these foods, including many others, there would be a somewhat drought of food! Way worse than than the California water drought even.

        Finally, we must know about what we can do about the coming drought. (One day.) Reverse Osmosis is how we turn ocean water, to drinking water! According to www.puretecwater.com, by CO. of PURETEC, 2012-2017, we currently have a Reverse Osmosis plant in Carlsbad, California. (Search it on Google.Maps if you wish!) And the earth is about 80% water so there is a lot of water to be able to use. And if global warming is true, (Don't scold me for not entirely believing) we can take the ocean water and keep the ocean at the same height. (Mostly.) Also, according to www.espwaterproducts.com, they say that Fluoride, Lead, Chlorine & Chloramine, Pesticides, Nitrates and Sulfates, Detergents and more are removed from water thanks to reverse osmosis. (Which makes the water that much healthier. Because, well, lead intake is obviously bad for you.)

Look, it makes sense that we shouldn’t need to be concerned about the drought anymore for the next drought may not come for another 1,200 years. But why put our descendants in this predicament or face this drought again in our near future? We almost ran out of water in 2015, farmers produce nearly half what the USA eats, and we can use ocean water as an extra source of fresh water! And now that this drought has seemingly come to an end, we can prepare for the next!

        We can fix this by changing some of our ways. First, since we almost ran out of water in our reserves, we should add more reserves. Then for farmers, they can use less water by irrigating it. (Drip irrigation.) And we can even add more reverse osmosis

facilities for fresh water incase the reserves start running out of water. So now that this drought starts to end and another to inevitably begin one day once again, we should fix what we did wrong last time, and be ready for next time because we have been given a second chance. And mistakes are what make us stronger.
