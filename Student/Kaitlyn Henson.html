How Fiction Inspires Us

We read fiction almost every day. Most of us are so used to it, we don’t even realize that fiction really changes us.

Stories are the only other thing besides us that understands our human complexity, like an old friend. When we put a book down, it gives us new understandings of the world around us, and changes who we are.

“The more deeply we are cast under a story’s spell, the more potent it’s influence,” - Thomas Ehrlich, Ernestine Fu

Nonfiction only states reality; facts the human brain needs to know to get through life. Scientists have claimed that we are more tense and skeptical when we read nonfiction.

With fiction, we ease our guard. And allow ourselves to get carried deep into a story. (Kristen Houghton)

“Fiction is obliged to stick to possibilities. Truth isn’t,” -Twain (author of Tom Sawyer)

Because fiction understands our complexity, it can lead us to do the great things we do today, through fiction. Fiction inspires us to be better people, and I think this is how fiction inspires us.
