Muslim Ban

        Everyday all over the world, the United States government makes decisions and takes actions that have serious consequences for many. The United States is heavily involved with many other countries through business, government, and military connections. The impact of all of these connections is serious and everyday actions have its issues and consequences. President Trump's executive orders placing travel restrictions on Muslim Majority countries, is one of the current issues that will change the lives of many. (www.cnn.com) The Muslim ban is an issue that we all need to pay attention to. President Trump’s actions make the United States appear as a country that can no longer been seen as the land of opportunity for immigrants. His argument that Muslims are terrorists who come here just to kill Americans isn’t true. His actions are causing more harm than good.

        Since the 1600’s, the colonists who settled in what now is the United States, struggled to accept the Native who had a different culture and religion. The colonists took their land and tried to make them Christians. The Native Americans were promised land and homes. That never happened. (Breakstone, Joel. "King Phillips War." (n.d.): n. pag. Sheg.stanford.edu. Web. 27 Apr. 2017.) Our current government is again discriminating against people who aren’t Christian and who has a different ethnicity. The actions decided by Donald trump continue the pattern started by the colonists.

        Our president has put the travel ban on Iraq, Iran, Sudan, Somalia, Libya, Syria, and yemen. The travel ban prevents people from these seven countries from entering the U.S. “We cannot allow a beachhead of terrorism to form in America. We cannot allow our nation to become a sanctuary for extremists.” (www.bostonglobe.com) Trump feels that if as more and more Muslim majority countries, terrorist groups are going to form and more Americans are going to die. He placed the ban on countries with a Muslim majority population that he claims have “extremists.”

        None of the terrorists who attacked the US during 9-11 came from any of the seven banned countries, however, the majority of the 9-11 terrorists were from a country that isn’t even listed on the travel ban list. On September 11th 2001, 15 out of the 19 hijackers came from Saudi Arabia causing approximately 2,700 deaths. Why wouldn’t Saudi Arabia be on the list, since one of the most tragic terrorist attacks came from that country? Wasn’t Trumps main point of having the travel ban to protect the United States?  According to www.commondreams.org Donald Trump and the Saudi government have many business interests in common. You have to wonder if this is why Saudi Arabia isn’t on the travel ban list.

        President Trump talks about the American deaths caused by Muslims as an excuse or reason for his travel ban. He doesn't mention that more Muslims have been killed by the United States military than Americans have been killed by Muslims. In Iraq for example, according to Watson Institute at Brown University at least 165,000 civilians have died from violence involving the United States and its allies since 2003. Just this week, the United States government thought that it was a good idea to launch 59 missiles at a Syrian military base (www.washingtonpost) killing seven people.

         These actions and many others prove that the United States is causing a lot of death and hate in the countries that we attack. Have our actions towards other countries put our country in danger? Maybe this is why the United States is a target for terrorists. Could we have given a reasons for terrorists to attack the United States? We have threatened and killed so many of their people that we have given these countries a reason to attack the United States.

The United States says that we value freedom, civil rights, and democracy, yet our government’s actions don't reflect this. This travel ban has been the peripeteia for many.  The ban has impacted the lives of innocent people in and outside of the United States. Mahmoud Hassan, an 18 year old engineering student, said, “My dreams are basically ruined.”  He and many college students in the U.S. fear that if they leave the country to visit their families, they won’t be able to return and continue  their studies in the U.S. They fear being separated forever from their families and friends. (www.express.co.uk) 14,135 students from the seven banned countries attend school in the United States. (www.nytimes.com) They are in this country legally and have student visas. Every single student in the U.S. has been screened. Other legal residents and green card holders are also affected by the travel ban. From (www.uscis.gov) “A Green Card holder is someone who has been granted authorization to live and work in the United States on a permanent basis.” These people have the legal rights to be in the U.S. and they are working for many people in the U.S. Many people from these countries need to travel for marriage, family or business, including businesses in the U.S. and can’t do so freely.  About 100,000 visa holders were temporarily prevented from entering the U.S and over 67,000 entries were blocked (www.vox.com). So many lives have been disturbed because of Donald Trump's decisions.

It is tragic that our president doesn't seem to understand that people are being hurt by his choices. If he is aware, he doesn't really seem to care. This is a tragedy because people are forced to choose between the people they love and their future. It is tragic that there so many killings have taken place in the Middle East as a result of the military involvement of the United States and its allies. The Muslim ban is an issues that we all need to pay attention to. This is an important and impactful issues of our time.
