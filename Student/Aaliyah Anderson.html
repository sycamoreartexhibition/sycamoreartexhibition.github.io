Proposed Health Care Bill

When Donald Trump was elected President by the electoral college, he had the lowest approval rating on record.  He still wanted to accomplish what he had promised his voters, to repeal and replace Obamacare.  He created Trumpcare, a health care that fits his needs and desires as well as people like him.  Trumpcare is a catastrophe, it follows the five stages of a tragedy.

Conservatives in the government have wanted to change Obamacare for a long time now.  When Trump was elected, he too was eager to change the health care plan in place into something that he and many people with the same financial status could benefit from.  Many right wing conservatives wanted to change Obamacare so it could be a choice and not the law to receive it.  

It is a hard and controversial question that I don't know the answer to.  Should it be the law to have health care?  Many would argue no.  But just to change that part of Obamacare takes time and effort.  :

“The plan also addresses problems for higher income Americans and gets rid of the fee.” (obamacarefacts.com/trumpcare-explained/) “If the proposed healthcare bill had passed, an analysis run by the Kaiser Family Foundation, seniors between the ages of 60 and 64 (people ages 65 and up qualify for Medicare) could wind up forking over thousands of dollars under Trumpcare”   : (TheMotleyFool.com/investing/)

 Older Americans would definitely benefit off of the proposed health care act.

.  “... the biggest beneficiary of all appears to be wealthier individuals.”        

¨An assessment made by the nonpartisan Joint Committee on Taxation found that people making $200,000 to $999,999 per year are in for a $274 billion tax break over the coming decade.  Another $157 billion will be saved by those earning $1 million or more.” (TheMotleyFool.com/investing/)

This new health care plan was made to benefit the rich.  Trumpcare was created to give trump and businessmen like him a tax cut.

Trumpcare was a tragedy, it follows the five stages of Aristotle's definition.  The first act: the character is happy, and at a height of their power.  The example of this would be on November 8th, when Trump was elected president.  The second act: a small problem or dilemma comes up.  An  example of that was when Trump realized he had to keep his promises to the public.  The third act: the problem becomes a greater issue but it can still be easily solved.  That happened when Trump was pressured to write a new health care plan as well as a new tax reform and to deal with ISIS.  The fourth act: the character has failed to avoid the problem and “impending is crisis or catastrophe”.  Trump rushed the health care bill, freaked out the public, and ended up not voting on the bill because they knew it wouldn’t pass.  The final and fifth act: shows the consequences of failure.  That happened when Trump was expected to start tax reform but people didn’t trust him anymore to go through with his promises.

Trumpcare would have hurt more people than it would have helped.  One thing it did help were drug stocks. The headlines to (money.cnn.com)  were:

“... investors are excited. Health care stocks are soaring” (La Monica 1)

“ Two big health care ETFS -- the Health Care Sector SPDR (XLV) and iShares U.S. Healthcare (IYH) funds -- are each up nearly 10% this year.  That’s better than the overall  market.”  

Because of the new proposed health care act, drug stocks rose very high making investors exited.

¨Ciga (CI) and Anthem (ANTM), which had hoped to merge, are up 14% and 17% respectively.  (Humana (HUM) and Aetna (AET), the other two that were looking to combine, are each up about 6%) “  ¨Drug makers Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) and Merk (MRK) are each up about 10% too.  Big biotech Amgen (AMGN) has soared 16%¨ -(money.cnn.com)

         All of these are examples are examples of stocks looking at big raises from between 6 and 17%.  That particular data is from 7 stocks who experience these dramatic changes.

        As for those who Trumpcare would have hurt, they’re not stocks, they’re people.  Trumpcare would have taken away health care

”from some 22 million people, … replacement plan for health insurance coverage, based on allowing companies to sell insurance state to state and charging the tax treatment of health care politics brought by individuals, would only help about 1 million (people)¨ - (trumpcare.com Health Network Group)  ¨They (CBO) currently suggest, Mr.Trump’s plan to repeal and replace Obamacare would cost between $270 billion over ten years with economic growth anticipated.” - (trumpcare.com)

Not only would it take so much work to have Americans switch to Trumpcare, it would cost hundreds of billions of dollars to do.

By knowing these things, you realize that Trumpcare would have probably hurt you, and 22 million other people like you.  You realize that in the making of this health care plan, the creators were focused on their needs, so the health plan would help people like them.  The replacement was rushed and needed more revision so that  Trump could please both sides as well as create a bill that would be a little more popular than the first draft of Trumpcare.
